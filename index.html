Projects

  • Factors affecting interest rates volitality in Kenya: Interest rates, international trade, and inflation influence foreign exchange rates in Kenya. Higher interest rates attract foreign investors, increasing demand for the Kenyan Shilling, while lower rates lead to reduced foreign capital inflows, causing depreciation. International trade affects currency values as a trade surplus strengthens the Shilling by increasing currency exchange for Kenyan goods, whereas a trade deficit raises demand for foreign currencies, leading to depreciation. Inflation impacts the Shilling’s value; high inflation reduces purchasing power and deters foreign investment, causing depreciation, while low inflation encourages investment and supports the currency’s stability in global markets. These factors show the connection between economic policies and foreign exchange rates.
